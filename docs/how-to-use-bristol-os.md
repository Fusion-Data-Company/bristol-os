# How to use Bristol OS (the only page you need)

Bristol OS turns Claude into a research-and-analysis teammate for Bristol Development Group. You don't need to learn commands. **Just open your Bristol workspace folder in Claude and talk in normal words.**

## The one rule
Tell Claude what you're trying to figure out — plainly — and it will do the research and build the document. It already knows your role and how Bristol works (that's the CLAUDE.md).

## Things you can say
- "Find and rank some submarkets for our next deal in [metro]."
- "Is [address or submarket] a good place to build? Give me a one-pager."
- "Pull rent comps for [submarket] and build me a market study."
- "Who owns this parcel?" / "Who's behind this LLC?"
- "Who could we raise this deal from? Build me a target investor list."
- "Research my underwriting assumptions for [deal] — rents, taxes, insurance, construction costs."
- "Turn all this into an investment committee memo."
- "What's in my pipeline? Give me a Monday status update."
- "Go deep on [any topic] and give me a sourced briefing."
- "What can you do?" (Claude shows the menu.)

## What you'll get
Real saved files in your workspace — memos, market studies, one-pagers, research briefings — not just chat. Everything important is sourced and dated.

## Keeping it sharp
- Add a market or deal: "Update my CLAUDE.md, I'm now tracking [deal/market]."
- Want deeper research tools? Say "set up deeper research" and Claude walks you through the free upgrades (Tavily, Exa) or Bristol's data tools.

## What it won't do (on purpose)
- It won't invent numbers — if it can't verify something, it tells you.
- It gives you the facts and analysis to decide; final calls and legal/tax/securities questions go to the right professional.

That's it. Open the folder, say what you need, get the work back.
