## What This Calibrates

Bristol Development Group builds **Class-A, amenity-rich, urban and suburban multifamily** (mid-rise and garden/podium apartment communities, plus a few for-sale/condo and townhome projects) in **growing Sunbelt and Southeast markets**. Typical projects are ~150–400 units with resort-style pools, Technogym fitness centers, co-working space, structured parking, and pet amenities — positioned for the upper end of each local rental market.

Bristol OS should therefore default to Bristol's **real geography and product type**, not a generic national footprint:

- **Core state:** Tennessee (half the portfolio), anchored by **Nashville** (their home market and largest cluster).
- **Primary expansion states:** Virginia (Richmond + Norfolk), Florida (Jacksonville corridor), Alabama (Huntsville, Birmingham, Mobile, Auburn), Kentucky (Louisville).
- **Newer/secondary states:** Texas (San Antonio, Austin) and Arkansas (Northwest Arkansas / Fayetteville).
- **Metro defaults, in priority order:** Nashville → Richmond → Knoxville → Jacksonville → Memphis → Louisville → Birmingham → Huntsville → Clarksville → Norfolk/Virginia Beach, then the remaining one-off Sunbelt metros above.
- **Product defaults:** Class-A multifamily, ~150–400 units/community, amenity-heavy, urban infill + suburban growth-corridor sites in the Sunbelt. Avoid defaulting to coastal-gateway, Midwest, or Northeast markets — Bristol does not operate there.
