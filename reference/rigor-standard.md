# Bristol OS — Rigor Standard (institutional / Berkshire-tier)

The audience operates at the top of the industry. Assume fluency; lead with judgment. Every analysis must carry these metrics and pass these checks — in V1, and tightened through V3 (see `deal-evolution`). This is the difference between "looks sophisticated" and "is."

## Return metrics (compute all that apply; never just margin)
- **Untrended yield-on-cost** (Year-1 stabilized NOI ÷ total cost) AND **trended YoC** (NOI at stabilization with rent growth) — state both; sponsors underwrite to both.
- **Development spread** = YoC − market exit cap, in bps — AND spread vs. **cost of capital** (blended debt+equity), not just vs. cap rate. A positive cap spread with a negative spread-to-cost-of-capital is not a deal.
- **Going-in cap** (if an acquisition) and **exit/reversion cap** (state the basis for each).
- **Levered IRR**, **equity multiple (MOIC)**, **cash-on-cash** (stabilized and average) over the hold. For merchant build, **development profit** and **margin on cost**; for hold, the 5–10yr levered return.
- **Return on cost vs. market cap** (the merchant-build spread) and **profit per unit**.

## Capital structure & debt
- LTC / LTV, **DSCR**, **debt yield** (NOI ÷ loan) — debt yield is the lender's real test; show it.
- Rate basis: quote vs. **SOFR** and the **10-yr UST**; note the **cap-rate-to-Treasury spread** and where it sits historically. Construction debt is floating — address **rate-cap cost** and **refinance/takeout risk** at stabilization.
- Sources & uses that foot to total cost; equity check; note recourse/guarantee posture if known.

## Basis & cost discipline
- Cost **per unit** and **per SF**; compare to **replacement cost** and to **recent sale comps ($/unit)** — "are we building below or above where we could buy?"
- Itemized hard/soft/land/parking/contingency. **Structured/underground parking is usually the swing** — break it out with $/space.
- Land basis as % of cost; note if contributed/owned (changes the return math).

## Revenue rigor
- Distinguish **market vs. in-place vs. achievable** rents; **$/SF** by unit type; **loss-to-lease**, **concessions** (asking vs. effective), **other-income ratio**, **trade-outs** on renewals/new leases.
- Rent comps must be **named, with address and $/SF**, not a blended guess. Absorption/lease-up pace and stabilized occupancy with basis.

## Market & macro context (always situate the deal in the cycle)
- **Supply pipeline**: units under construction + planned vs. trailing absorption in the submarket — the #1 multifamily risk. Name competing deliveries.
- Demand drivers: jobs, in-migration, household formation, income, the specific employment base.
- Rate/cycle backdrop: current 10-yr UST, SOFR, where cap rates sit vs. history, transaction-volume trend. Tie the recommendation to the rate path.

## Stress & sensitivity (mandatory)
- **Base / downside / upside**, flexing rent, cost, exit cap, and rate together — and show where the deal **goes negative**. A model that can't lose money in the downside isn't credible.
- Break-even occupancy and break-even rent. Identify the 2–3 variables the deal is most sensitive to.

## Local accuracy (non-negotiable — they will catch you)
- Get the **jurisdiction, submarket, corridor, school zone, zoning, and entitlement status exactly right.** For Franklin/Williamson: verify against primary sources (city planning/HZC, county assessor, the developer's own filings). A wrong school district or mislabeled submarket destroys credibility instantly.
- Verify any third-party project/owner attribution before stating it (e.g., confirm a project is the one you think it is — don't conflate two Franklin developments).

## Honesty & sourcing (the trust layer)
- Every figure: **sourced fact `[S#]`**, **labeled estimate**, or **"per Bristol underwriting."** Never present an estimate as fact.
- Confirm material figures across **2+ independent sources**; flag single-source items.
- Reconcile every number across the model, memo, deck, and one-pager. Every `[S#]` resolves to a real URL in `sources.md`.
- State assumptions explicitly with their basis. Where data is thin, say so and give a range — don't fabricate precision.

## The bar
If a 30-year principal or a Berkshire-level allocator would read it and find one lazy number, one missing metric, or one wrong local detail — it's not done. That's the standard for V3, every time.
